Two kids, mid-to-late teens, from one earlier relationship that ended before 2017. Un-villainized co-parent (treat her like the 2019 ex), ~50/50. Kids are an ongoing fact of his life, never his redemption arc. Revision: avoid hard birth years — they collided with the locked timeline; "mid/late teens" is safe.

2. Where the bad self-talk was installed ✓ kept clean

The father's voice installs it — bridge line: "I became my father's voice. I taught myself he was right." The 2017–19 relationship reinforces, doesn't create it. Connects the two locked canon lines exactly.

3. The father today revised

Estrangement as ongoing, ambiguous grief — no hope of reconciliation, relief and loss side by side. "Forgiveness ≠ reconciliation" as the permission layer. No "closure," no tidy hug.

4. The mother & his faith revised

She's the bedrock source of his faith, internalized from childhood — surfaces in his worst years but does not fix him. Can be alive & present (avoids the dead-saint trap), but not his wound-healer and never corrects his spiral.

5. His work / livelihood revised

Skilled trades — carpentry/electrical, apprentice → journeyman in his 20s/30s; now a foreman / site-supervisor or property-maintenance lead. The drinking years hurt the work but it stayed a phase. Cut the recovery/sober-living job entirely.

6. The drinking ✓ verified

A bounded, situational coping phase (lost routines + isolation + grief), not addiction. Ban all "I was an alcoholic" language. The turn comes from exhaustion — the drinking is just one failed patch.

7. The bridge to "the map" (the quiz) revised

Witness / sitting-first — the map = field notes from years of ordinary one-on-one conversation after his solitary turn ("what I kept hearing"), not "the system I built from my pain." Never a facilitator, counselor, or credentialed peer.
