Why listen to me

I didn't read about this. I lived it.

My phone rang by accident. I wasn't meant to be on that call. I stayed on it for twenty-two minutes — long enough to hear the woman I was going to marry planning a life I wasn't in. I ended it that day.

Then I erased her. Every photo. Every message. Gone in a week. Except one voicemail — twenty-six seconds I could never make myself delete. I played it at two in the morning for a year.

People keep asking me what's on that voicemail. They're asking the wrong question.

2am · the house finally quiet

You've carried it so long, you think it's just who you are now.

The thing someone did to you years ago. The love you gave that never came back the same. The loss nobody else still talks about. Or maybe no one did anything at all — you just kept shrinking, putting everyone first, never quite feeling like enough. You learned to function around it. You kept going. You told yourself other people have it worse.

But at 2am, when the house is quiet, the weight is still there — and you're tired of pretending it isn't.

Most people think the only way to put it down is to excuse what happened, let the person back in, or just keep trying harder to be enough. So they never put it down at all.

What the weight really costs

You can climb carrying all of it.
You just can't climb free.

The weight doesn't sit still — it grows. Every year you carry it up the hill, the next step gets heavier, until one day you start calling the exhaustion your life.

The one thing to remember

It was never a gift you gave them.
It's the weight you set down for yourself.

Before you start

Isn't forgiving just letting them off the hook?

No. You can forgive someone completely and never let them near you again. Forgiveness sets you free — it was never a key back to the door.

Do I have to be religious for this?

No. Faith is woven through what I share because it's woven through me — but the quiz, the reset, and the daily meet you wherever you are.

What's the daily, and is it free?

The Daily Rebuild is a short message from me each day — video, text, and one small action. It's free, it lives on Substack, and you can leave anytime.

Will you flood my inbox?

Only what you choose. Your quiz result, the seven-day reset, and the daily if you want it. Unsubscribe anytime; your information is never shared.
